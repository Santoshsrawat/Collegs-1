Kasturba Medical College Manipal

Dr. T. M. A. Pai founded Kasturba Medical College (KMC) in 1953, Since then it has been delivering quality education to medical aspirants in the country. The General Medical Council of Great Britain and the Medical Council of India (MCI) have both recognized KMC. The College was the first private medical college in India when it was established in 1953.

KMC is known for its excellent medical education and research facilities. The college offers undergraduate (MBBS), postgraduate (MD/MS), and super-specialty (DM/MCh) courses in various disciplines of medicine. It also offers diploma courses in nursing and other paramedical fields.

KMC has tie-ups with several international universities, and its students have the opportunity to pursue research and clinical electives abroad.

The faculty of KMC has been dedicated to providing students with high-quality medical education and training. The college encourages its faculty and students to participate in research activities and has a robust research program. Dr. H. Sudarshan Ballal, a renowned nephrologist and Chairman of Manipal Hospitals, and Dr. Vivek Murthy, who was the Surgeon General of the United States, are two notable alumni of KMC.

Kasturba Medical College Manipal Location Details

The town of Manipal in the Udupi area of Karnataka, India is home to Kasturba Medical College. Mangalore, a major Karnataka city, is about 65 kilometers north of Manipal. In the center of Manipal, on a 200-acre expanse, the campus is easily reached by road and rail. The Mangalore International Airport is the closest airport to Manipal. The town of Manipal is well-connected by road and rail to major cities in Karnataka and neighboring states.

Kasturba Medical College Manipal Seat Details 

Kasturba Medical College offers various undergraduate and postgraduate medical courses. However, it offers 250 seats for its MBBS program.

Counseling Process of Admission for MBBS in Karnataka

There are a total 3 rounds in the Karnataka NEET UG counseling, initial 2 rounds will be conducted online and the last round is done offline. Which is also called the Mop-Up round. 

Steps for admission process in Medical Colleges of Karnataka are as follows:

  • Eligibility: Candidates should meet the standard of qualifying criteria that is positioned by the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA), that include minimum age, educational qualifications, and qualifying exam scores.
  • Application: Candidates have  to apply online via. the KEA website under the defined period. They need to upload their educational documents and pay the application fee.
  • Document verification : In the next further step, candidates' documents get verified via. online.
  • Merit List: The KEA releases a merit list according to the NEET scores of the candidates. This merit list is used to assign ranks to the candidates.
  • Choice Filling : Choice filling is a critical and an essential step in the college admission process. It's important to note that choice filling is only available during Round 1 of the admission process. During Round 2, candidates can only reshuffle or delete colleges from their list. They cannot add any new colleges.
  • Allotment of Seat : Once a candidate has completed the choice filling process, the next step in the college admission process is the seat allotment process. This is the stage where colleges allocate seats to the candidates based on their rank, category, and the availability of seats.
  • Reporting Process : If you have been allotted a college through the seat allotment process and are satisfied with the college allotted to you, it is mandatory to report to the college within the specified time frame. 
  • Choice entry after allotment : Choice 1 
  • Admission: After being allotted a seat, candidates need to report to the college under the given time period (make sure you plan things in advance, as there are cities which takes time to reach), have to complete the admission formalities, and pay the remaining fees.
