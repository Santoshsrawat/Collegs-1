Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose Medical College Jabalpur

Named after the renowned freedom fighter Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose, the Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose Medical College has a rich history as it is affiliated with the Rani Durgavati Vishwavidyalaya, Jabalpur, and is recognized by the Medical Council of India (MCI). 

Offering UG, PG, and Diploma courses, the college has a dedicated and highly qualified faculty team that includes experienced professors, doctors, and researchers. The faculty members engage in research activities, contributing to the advancement of medical knowledge and the development of innovative healthcare practices.

The campus provides a conducive environment for learning and practical training, ensuring that students receive a comprehensive medical education.

NSCB Medical College Jabalpur has its associated teaching hospital, Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose Medical College Hospital. It is equipped with advanced diagnostic and treatment facilities, specialized departments, and emergency services. The hospital serves as a training ground for students, providing them with hands-on clinical experience and exposure to diverse medical cases.

NSCB Medical College Jabalpur holds a prominent position in the healthcare landscape of Madhya Pradesh. It strives to produce competent medical professionals who contribute significantly to the healthcare industry and society as a whole. 

NSCB Medical College Location Details

The college is situated on Tilwara Road, in the Medical College Colony area of Jabalpur. It is easily accessible within the city and is located in close proximity to transportation hubs.

Jabalpur has a good transportation infrastructure. Jabalpur Junction is the main railway station serving the city, connecting it to various cities and towns across India through regular train services. The nearest airport to Jabalpur is Dumna Airport, located approximately 20 kilometers away. The airport provides domestic flights and connects Jabalpur to major cities in India.

Admission Process

Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose Medical College Jabalpur

The admission process for MBBS courses in Madhya Pradesh is conducted by the Directorate of Medical Education, Madhya Pradesh. There are 13 Govt/ 1 AIIMS & 9 Private medical colleges in Madhya Pradesh which offer 2704 MBBS seats. 

Counseling is conducted for both Government as well as Private Medical Colleges.

It is important to know that non-domicile students will only be considered for admission. If any seats are left vacant after accommodating all eligible domicile candidates, they will be filled as per the college's admission policies.

Also, Madhya Pradesh has the advantage of being more affordable for MBBS courses that are comparatively lower to other states, which makes it an affordable destination for students who want to pursue medical education without incurring high expenses. 

You can have proper knowledge, about the entire MBBS admission process, and step-by-step procedures:

Counseling registration process-

Step 1 -  Candidate Registration -  Only single registration is applicable which happens only before the first round of counseling.

Step 2 - Application form Filling - Fill out the application form with all the necessary information.

Step 3 - Upload Document- You have to upload all the necessary educational  documents.

Step 4 - Fee Payment - Pay the fee for counseling either online or through Net Banking/ Credit Card/ Debit card.

The allotment Process in Madhya Pradesh includes

The allotment process for colleges in Madhya Pradesh starts with an online counseling process. Once you complete this, you will need to register for counseling after which a merit list will be published on the official website and a counseling schedule will be made available online. 

Students will then need to choose their preferred colleges in order of priority. After a week, the seat allotment list will be published. If you are allotted a seat in the first round, you must confirm your admission by submitting original documents to the college and paying the admission fee online before the deadline.

Counseling Rounds ;

The Directorate of Madhya Pradesh Medical Education conducts three rounds of counseling. These are :

  1. First Round counseling
  2. Second Round Counseling
  3. Mop - Up Round

If there are any stray vacancies left in the college after the Mop-up round, then the merit list will be sponsored by the college. Also, if new candidates are interested in sponsoring, they will need to register separately in addition to the existing candidates.

Things to remember, to avoid mistakes :

If a candidate fails to confirm their admission during the first round, they will lose their seat, but their fee will not be forfeited. However, candidates who want to participate in the second or mop-up round will need to pay an additional fee online. 

Those who pay the second round fee will only need to pay a different amount to participate in the mop-up round.

If a candidate is allotted a seat in the second or mop-up round, they will need to deposit their original documents for verification to confirm their admission. If they fail to do so, they will forfeit both their allotted seat and their fees.

Seat Upgradation :

  1. Selected candidates in the first round can participate in the second round by submitting a wish for up-gradation during the admission process and by payment of second-round fees.
  2. If candidates upgrade their admission, they have to pay a different admission fee online, but they will lose the admission processing fee that was submitted earlier by them.

Resignation :

Candidates who resign from their admitted seat before the start of the second round will only forfeit 10% of their admission fees or Rs. 10,000, whichever is higher, as a processing charge. However, if they resign after the start of the second round, they will face a penalty that may include the entire course fees or the amount specified in the bond.

Eligibility to Participate in MP State Medical Counseling

To be eligible to apply, candidates must have scored at least 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. Additionally, as of 31st December 2021, candidates should have completed 17 years of age. However, for candidates belonging to the SC/ST category, they must have secured a minimum of 40% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. Candidates with disabilities (PWD) should have obtained at least 15% marks in the same subjects.
