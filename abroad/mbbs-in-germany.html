Germany is regarded as one of the ideal places to study MBBS as it is home to numerous prestigious universities that are known worldwide. Being the most welcoming and safest nation with a wide range of educational opportunities and practical studies, it draws the attention of many international students.

Still unable to convince yourself to study MBBS in Germany?

Then, after reading the entire blog, you will realise how advantageous it is to study in a developed country, which is also the third most popular destination.

Do you know that in a highly developed country like Germany, you can study MBBS for free?

Yes, you heard that right!  MBBS programme in Germany provides free tuition fees as well as a high level of living to aspire doctors. Not only this but Germany provides its medical students a lot more than this, including excellent exposure to international students and top-notch education also.

So, let's get started and learn everything about MBBS in Germany in detail to have a better understanding of the benefits to study MBBS in Germany, the admission process and other necessary aspects that a medical aspirant should not miss.

Top Universities

Why

It's interesting to note that the term "MBBS" is not used in Germany; instead, Medizen is the term used. Let's move on and learn why MBBS in Germany will be a wise choice.

Studying MBBS in Germany is merely the best destination to lead your career in medicine. Here are the reasons for the same:-

  1. Free Education

Germany is the most desired country for students to study MBBS since education is free at all public universities. However, there is a condition that goes along with this great benefit. So, you must perform well in 12th board exam. If you score 90 or higher, it will be quite easy for you to get accepted into the best public universities of Germany. However, it will be challenging but not impossible if your score is in the 80s. As in Germany, you will be allotted a medical institution based on your grade from your 12th grade as well as your FSP score.

This is the main feature that stands out and makes many students want to study in Germany and achieve great marks is that education there is free.

  1. Quality of Life

Another advantage of studying MBBS in Germany is that, in addition to having a very low cost of living, it also allows future doctors to live the kind of life they wish. As Germany offers a vibrant nightlife, clubs, and restaurants, as well as throughout the entire year festivals and events that improve the quality of life for students.

Moreover, Germany, a global leader in the medical sector, provides medical students with cutting-edge technologies to improve their hands-on learning. A student is therefore exposed to a high level of quality life, starting with free schooling and continuing with excellent education. And no doubt, Germany is undoubtedly an advanced nation, and it offers everything of the highest calibre to its medical aspirants.

  1. Stay Back Facility

Following the six-year MBBS programme, students have the option of staying in Germany for an additional 18 months. The German government offers this service in order to give students the chance to stay in Germany and find employment so they can establish themselves and pursue careers in Germany. Therefore, Germany is highly recommended for students who have made the decision to study and settle abroad because there are strong career opportunities with competitive salary packages.

  1. Work Permit is Granted

In Germany, prospective doctors are permitted to work part-time and cover their own expenses. Because of this, foreign students are permitted to work up to 20 hours per week or 120 full days per year. For students who need money or want to work in addition to their MBBS studies, this is a wonderful alternative.

  1. Travel Europe

As Germany is located in the centre of Europe, studying there enables medical students to travel to other nations like France and the Netherlands. As a result, it is a good option for international students who are outspoken and prefer to socialize.

It is anticipated that you are now persuaded to study MBBS in Germany after learning about all these benefits. Let's study the eligibility requirements now in order to adequately prepare for it.

Intake

There are two deadlines for admission each semester, one in the summer and one in the winter. The deadline for summer admissions is 15th January for courses starting in April. While the deadline for the winter intake is between 15th May and 15 June, and the course starts in October.

Duration of MBBS in Germany

It takes a total of 6 years to complete MBBS in Germany. In which the first two years ( 4 semesters) of pre-clinical is taught. In the next 3 years clinical is focused. And 1 year of internship is involved.

Planning your budget

How much does it cost to study in the MBBS In Germany?

The tuition fee charges to study MBBS in Germany which is actually the semester fees is between 250 euro to 450 euro per semester. However in one state specifically named as Baden Wurttemberg, the semester fee is around 1500 euro per semester.

Admission Process

Step 1: Select the University according to your interest
Step 2:  Check the eligibility criteria of the university as every university has different criteria and requirements.
Step 3: Fill out the application form
Step 4: Arrange the documents and submit them on the website
Step 5: After receiving the confirmation of your selection, pay the university and its enrollment fees
Step 6: It’s now time to visit German Embassy for visa.

Eligibility Criteria for MBBS in Germany

  1. The German language is the most important factor. It is essential to learn German because just three MBBS colleges in Germany provide the programme in English; all other institutions offer the programme in German. Admission into any of the three medical colleges providing in MBBS in Germany is competitive due to the high level of competition already present. Therefore, any medical student who wants to pursue a career in medicine must begin learning German in the eleventh grade and finish at the C1 level.
     
  2. The second prerequisite for enrolling at German colleges is to achieve a score of 90 or higher in the 12th board. So, you must obtain a good score to meet the requirements and be eligible to enroll in medical school in Germany. However, if your score is lower than 90 or even in the 80s, it indicates that some effort you will be required to study MBBS in Germany. Having an MBBS Abroad Consultant on your side can help you give guidance on how to do well in the admission exam and get into German medical schools
     
  3. Before beginning to pursue MBBS, students must complete a one-year foundation course after completing their 12th-grade year, which comprises two semesters conducted in the German language. The term "Studienkolleg" is commonly used to refer to the first year. Due to intense competition and lack of seats, this exam is regarded as one of the hardest to pass.  

M Kurs Studienkolleg – There are different types of Studienkolleg, but for MBBS, it is the M Course, which is suitable for students of dentistry, medicine, veterinary medicine, pharmacy, biochemistry, bioinformatics, biology, and other, biological or pharmaceutical degree programmes.

  1. An entrance exam known as the "Aufnahmeprufung" must be passed in order to enroll in the foundation course (Studienkolleg). The purpose of the admission exam is to evaluate a student's competency in German and mathematics.

All in all, you should start learning German in 11th grade. With a strong 12th-grade record and a solid command in the German language, you must then prepare for the Aufnahmeprufung. Once you pass it, you are then qualified to take the M Kurs Studienkolleg, and the final test, the Festellungsprufung (FSP), is the last one.

As a result, admission occurs by combining the 12th-grade score with the FSP score. The average of both is regarded as a German GPA.

In Germany, a score for MBBS is provided on a scale from 1 to 5, rather than a percentage. To determine which grade indicates a good score and which a negative score, let's look at the German grading scale.

Documents Required for MBBS in Germany:

  • Passport with 18 months of validity
  • 10th and 12th marksheet
  • Birth Certificate
  • 10 passport size photographs
  • German language proficiency proof
  • Offer letter of the Medical University of Germany
  • Authorisation of all documents from the Ministry of External Affairs
  • Legalisation of documents from German Embassy

Our FAQ's

1. How much does it cost for MBBS in Germany?

In Germany, a yearly cost of living of Rs. 10,400 is calculated. And because there is no tuition price and only a semester fee is due, it comes out to about 300 Euros every semester.

2. Is Germany MBBS valid in India?

Yes, MBBS degrees are only recognised in India upon successful completion of the FMGE i.e.Foreign Graduate Examination.

3. Can I do MBBS in Germany after 12th?

All Indian students who successfully complete their 12th grade with cumulative grades of at least 85 in all six subjects are eligible to apply for MBBS in Germany.

4. Is MBBS hard in Germany?

Obviously, MBBS in Germany is challenging because a student must achieve scores over 90 in their 12th grade in order to be eligible for admission to one of the country's top medical schools.

5. Is MBBS in Germany safe for Indian students?

Germany proves to be the safest nation since it is a highly developed nation with the lowest crime rate when compared to other nations. In addition to this, institutions ensure that all students are taken care of by establishing tight guidelines and placing CCTV cameras throughout the campus.
