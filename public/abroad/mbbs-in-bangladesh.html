In the recent scenario, the enrollment of medical aspirants for MBBS in Bangladesh is rising. Bangladesh has emerged as a priority place to pursue MBBS for aspiring Indian MBBS aspirants. 

Passing NEET is mandatory for Indian candidates seeking MBBS admission in Bangladesh, and there shouldn't be a gap of more than two years when applying for an MBBS course in Bangladesh after the 12th class. All these conditions have been implemented by Bangladesh to pursue MBBS in their country. 

Also, Bangladesh reserves 104 seats in its medical universities for SAARC member countries students. SAARC is a regional organization of south asian countries, and India is also a member of the SAARC organization.

Details of Bangladesh MBBS colleges -

There are 75 private MBBS colleges in Bangladesh and 37 government medical colleges. Thus enrolling in one of them can help you become a skilled healthcare professional. 

Out of a total of 75 universities, in Bangladesh, there are 25 public universities and 50 private universities for medical education. The MBBS in Bangladesh is an economical option for Indian students seeking to study medicine abroad. Bangladesh can be considered an affordable option to pursue an MBBS program compare to any neighboring country of India.

Indian students should choose to study MBBS in Bangladesh because the standard of education in Bangladesh is excellent. 

One can easily assess the superior quality of education in Bangladesh by looking at the remarkable pass rates of MBBS graduates from Bangladesh. 

There are some colleges in Bangladesh located in amazing locations with greater connectivity with the city.  Many famous medical universities in Bangladesh are recognized by WHO and NMC. Some characteristics of Bangladesh medical college-

  • A good number of Patients in-flow
  • Brilliant Infrastructure
  • Supportive Learning environment 
  • Organized and systematic culture
  • The Majority of colleges have a rich history and extensive experience

  1. There are a total of 25% of seats reserved for Indian students, in Bangladesh. That means pursuing MBBS in Bangladesh has the potential for more seat allotment possibilities, meaning you may have a better chance of selection.
  2. As India is a member of the South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation (SAARC), students from India studying in Bangladesh do not need to pay tuition fees as per the provision for SAARC member nations.
  3. The close distance between India and Bangladesh provides a unique chance for students to reach Bangladesh in a short time. By taking a one-hour flight from Kolkata, students can easily reach Bangladesh.

  7. Students get a chance of doing internships for 1 year in many prestigious colleges.
  8. Obtaining approval for a visa is a simple process.
  9. There is direct admission for MBBS in Bangladesh, without any donation. 
  10. Similar culture for Indian students whether it is Culture, Food, Climate, or diaspora.

The nations of India and Bangladesh are geographically connected by a land border, which facilitates convenient travel between the two countries. Also, if you wish to travel by air you can travel from Kolkata to Bangladesh by air in just one hour.

Admission Process

The process for MBBS admission process can go through these steps -

  1. Counseling and Admission: Students who clear the entrance exam are called for counseling and admission process. During the counseling, students need to submit their original documents, pay the admission fees, and complete other formalities to secure a seat.
  2. Visa: Indian students need to apply for a student visa to study MBBS in Bangladesh. They need to submit the admission letter, passport, and other required documents to the Bangladesh embassy to obtain a student visa.
Note - Among all these processes, you have to qualify for the NEET to prove your eligibility.

 

Eligibility Criteria for MBBS Admission in Bangladesh 

Here is the Criteria condition requirement for admission -

  • Candidate should be NEET qualified.
  • To verify, if you meet the requirements for studying MBBS in Bangladesh, your cumulative grade point averages from both your 10th and 12th grades will be considered collectively.  (Minimum GPA needed is 6.5)
  • There should be no gap of more than 2 years after completing the 12th education.
  • The student should have 60% marks in the 12th standard.
  • Interested candidates have to clear the NEET exam with good marks.
  • Age must be above 17 years.

Is MBBS in Bangladesh Valid as per FMGL regulation 2021?

Yes, according to the Foreign Medical Graduate Screening Examination (FMGE) regulation of 2021, an MBBS degree obtained from a recognized medical university in Bangladesh is valid for Indian citizens who wish to practice medicine in India after clearing the FMGE.  

If a person intends to commence medical practice in Bangladesh, it is imperative that they possess a work visa as clinical practice is prohibited on a student visa.

FMGL Conditions by the National Medical Commission cleared it earlier, that a student has to pass the medical practicing licensing exam of India before he starts to practice medicine in INDIA. It has made it mandatory for every student who completed medicine from any country.

Conclusion

Bangladesh can be an ideal destination to pursue MBBS, but it has some disadvantages too such as - 

  1. In the first year of your MBBS program, you have to make a payment of approximately 40% of the total fee which may become unaffordable for some students.
  2. In Bangladesh, there is a strict rule that allows a maximum gap of only 2 years between high school and starting their MBBS program.
  3. The overall cost of Medical education is on a higher side compared to some overseas MBBS destinations. 
  4. Bangladesh is a little conservative society so students may find some restrictions to daily lifestyle vis a vis other countries. 
  5. Food can be an issue in some medical colleges located in offshore locations, especially for Indian students with a lack of access to Wheat and North Indian food options. 
  6. Bangladesh has some restrictions on clothes on their campuses.

Our FAQ's

Is Bangladesh good For MBBS?

The cost is on the higher side, but the level of medical education is the best among the Indian neighboring countries.

What is the total cost of pursuing MBBS?

In Bangladesh, there is the average total cost of pursuing an MBBS ranges from 30 lakhs to 45 lakhs.

Are there criteria for a job after doing MBBS from Bangladesh?

The Medical Council of India recognizes medical universities in Bangladesh, which means that after completing an MBBS program there, doctors can pursue a medical career or clinical practice in India. However, before starting their practice, they need to clear the FMGE MCI/NEXT NMC screening test.

Does MBBS of Bangladesh is valid in India?

Most of the medical universities of Bangladesh are recognized by the Medical Council of India (MCI) and WHO. Aspirants from India after completing MBBS from Bangladesh can start their medical career in India after clearing FMGE MCI / NEXT exam.

Does Bangladesh have a special Quota for Indian students?

Bangladesh welcomes Indian students to pursue MBBS courses, and they are offered seats in medical colleges under the SAARC member quota.

Are there any additional exams required for admission to Bangladesh?

No, you just have to fulfill the eligibility criteria. There is no additional exam.
