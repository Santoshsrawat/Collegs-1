It has always been every medical student's expectation to go to a college that is not only affordable but also good in terms of education, infrastructure, and teaching faculty. 

Nowadays, we have seen a trend among Indian MBBS aspirants, to explore options to pursue MBBS outside of India. Every year many Indian students go abroad to pursue a smooth MBBS career among these destinations Nepal is one of these. 

Here, are some Quick Facts about Nepal Medical College-

Recognised NMC and WHO Aproved College
Total Number of Colleges 20
Cost 45-60 lakh
Medium Of Education General English
IELTS/TOEFL Not Requied
Maximum Strengh 100 Seats In Most of The Colleges

 

Note: You must pass the NEET in the general category in order to be admitted to the MBBS program in Nepal. Admission is based on general category rank.

If you have ever considered pursuing MBBS in Nepal, then you are in for a great experience during your years of study because of the following reasons-

  • Nepal shares a border with India and it is very comfortable to reach there through the road network. Air travel to Nepal is also a good option with an average travel time of 1.45 hours. 
  • If parents are not in favor to send their children to study in a country that is far away from India, in that case, Nepal could be a very good alternative for them.
  • There is no requirement for a visa, for visiting and pursuing MBBS in Nepal which makes it easy for candidates to visit the campus in Nepal as well before enrolling.
  • Currency of Nepal and India is the same in purchasing power, 1 Nepalese rupee is equal to 0.63 rupees. So, the expenses will be the same as in India.
  • As both countries share a common cultural heritage and have a similar population sharing a Roti Beti relationship. For a student visiting Nepal is the same like visiting any other state of our country with language, food, and cultural similarities. 
  • Due to similar climatic conditions the diseases in both nations are the same. Studying MBBS in Nepal can provide you with an advantage when you will return back to India, as you will have already gained knowledge about the diseases prevalent in the region.
  • MBBS in Nepal follows all the rules and guidelines that have been set up by National Medical Commission. The course duration is the same as in INDIA, (4.5 years and 1-year internship).  Also, Nepal provides licenses to practice to foreign nationals post qualifying Nepal Medical Licencing Exam. Moreover, the syllabus and curriculum is similar of both India & Nepal.  
  • English is the language of MBBS and native people of Nepal are also fluent in Hindi.
  • While pursuing MBBS in Nepal, you will also be granted a license to practice medicine in Nepal. This presents an opportunity to establish yourself in Nepal if you wish to do so. However, when you return back to India, it is mandatory, for you to pass the NEXT exam to start practicing medicine in India.

Well, all these are some of the benefits and more detailed reviews we have done further in the article.

Why

The atmosphere for learning in Nepal is among the best in the world. The cultural similarities between India and Nepal provide an excellent opportunity for Indian students to integrate themselves into the local community. In recent years, the graph for Students who pursue MBBS in Nepal has increased a lot.

Additionally, Nepal has a positive and friendly relationship with India, further reducing any concerns about the safety of students studying in Nepal. 

The security arrangements for Indian students have consistently been of high quality and have been strengthened over time, providing a safe and secure environment for them to pursue their education.

The cost for MBBS in Nepal is affordable and you can expect a good learning experience if you have a budget of around 50 to 60 lakhs for your entire MBBS course.

Students have a lot of freedom to learn and grow more with the different cultures of students. The people of Nepal are very friendly and provide support to the students. While Nepal and India share many cultural similarities, there is a notable difference that can be felt. However, overall, both countries are quite similar in terms of culture.

Reasons for the Popularity of Nepal among Indian Students -

Tuition fee - The tuition fee for medical students is much more affordable for middle-income background students.

Strong clinical Exposure - The patient flow of Nepal’s colleges is much higher due to its large population. 

Passport is not required - There is no requirement for a passport for any Indian students that are seeking for MBBS in Nepal.

A safe environment - Nepal is a very warm and friendly country and the crime rate is almost nil in this nation also there is no racial discrimination.

Language is no barrier - You can easily communicate with the native people of Nepal, as Hindi is well-spoken and understandable by Nepal’s people. 

Convenient Weather - Nepal is not a country, where you find harsh climatic conditions, the weather is very normal and moderate. The minimum temperature ranges between 3 to 4 degrees Celsius in winter and 20 degrees in summer.

Good Transport connectivity - Nepal is very well connected with India,  not just by air. road and train too. 

Regulatory Body - Nepal has a regulatory body (Nepal Medical Commission) to ensure the best medical education.

Availability of Indian Food - There are certainly similarities in the dishes and ingredients used in both countries' foods of Nepal and India.

Many of the colleges have their own 500 + bed hospitals, which is a plus point for the students pursuing MBBS in Nepal. 

Also, Medical education rules and standards are constantly evolving, including the standards set by the National Medical Commission (NMC). These changes can sometimes cause difficulties for students pursuing medical education. However, Nepal is not affected by these changes as it has its own governing bodies and regulations for students Seeking to pursue MBBS in NEPAL.  

Both India and Nepal face similar nature’s health challenges and diseases. Therefore, students studying in Nepal will not face any difficulties in learning about healthcare and medical practices that are applicable in India as well.

Also, Nepal shares a border with India, which provides a convenient way for parents to reach out to their children or call them back if necessary. 

One of the biggest worries for parents, when their children are away for studies, is safety. Well, this is not the case with Nepal as it has a low crime rate and we have not seen any type of racial discrimination in Nepal. 

Several major cities in India, such as Delhi and Patna, are well-connected to major cities in Nepal, including Kathmandu. This makes it easier for parents to visit their children's campuses and check the conditions and study environment to ensure their children's career growth.

The mental and emotional well-being in Nepal has always been very highly lifted up for everyone else.

How much does it cost to study in the MBBS In Nepal?

The government of Nepal has set the tuition fee for students at 75,000 USD. Some colleges in Nepal offer scholarships based on the NEET score. The advantage of this system is that the tuition fee is fixed in Indian currency and does not vary. 

Additionally, there are no fees for visas or passports. So you can save more on transport charges. Also, apply for a bank loan in Nepal, if you genuinely want to do MBBS in Nepal.

The total cost in Nepal for Pursuing MBBS is around 50 to 65 lakh according to the college. The cost of living is also less.

MEEE EXAM Eligibility for MBBS in Nepal

When it comes to admission many students often find admission procedures confusing and one of the biggest questions for them is “Does every college in Nepal mandates clearing the MECEE exam for admission conducted by Nepal Medical Commission for local students. The answer is NO as this exam is not required by every college, but certain colleges like B. P. Koirala have it as a requirement for admission.

To have a clear idea about MBBS in Nepal, it is important to consult the best MBBS counselor, who have on-site campus visit in Nepal.  

What is the MCCEE exam?

MECEE is a national-level entrance exam held in Nepal for admission into medical and dental colleges.  It assesses students' knowledge and aptitude in subjects such as physics, chemistry, biology, and mathematics using a multiple-choice format with 200 questions to answer in three hours.

Is MBBS in Nepal Valid as per FMGL regulation 2021?

Yes, according to the Foreign Medical Graduate Screening Examination (FMGE) regulation of 2021, an MBBS degree obtained from a recognized medical university in Nepal is valid for Indian citizens who wish to practice medicine in India after clearing the FMGE. 

FMGL conditions by the National Medical Commission cleared it earlier, that a student has to pass the medical practicing licensing exam of India before he starts to practice medicine in India. NMC has made it mandatory for every student to fulfill FMGL conditions to be eligible to write the NEXT exam post returning back from Abroad.

As per the new norms Nepal Medical Commission is allowing Foreign Medical students to appear for Nepal Medical Licensing Exam which previously was not allowed. So Indian students have to now qualify for this exam in order to come back to INDIA and have to appear in the NEXT exam  (National Level Exit Test) exam.
