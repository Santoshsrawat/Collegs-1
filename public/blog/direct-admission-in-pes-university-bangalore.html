PES University Admission

Admission process for getting into PES University involves-

Entrance Exams - PES University annually conducts its own entrance exam for a variety of courses called PESSAT (PES Scholastic Aptitude Test). Additionally, PES University accepts results from KCET (Karnataka Common Entrance Test) and COMEDK (Consortium of Medical, Engineering, and Dental Colleges of Karnataka) scores for some programs, such as B.Tech.

Merit-Based Admission - Admission to PES University is often determined by a mix of academic performance and results on entrance exams. Candidates are shortlisted for subsequent rounds if they achieve the required cutoff marks.

Counseling/Interview - As part of the admissions process, shortlisted individuals might be contacted for counseling or interviews. This process enables the university to determine whether the applicants are qualified for the chosen program.

Direct Admission - In certain cases, PES University may grant students direct admission based on their achievement in prerequisite tests (such as class 12 or equivalent) and other requirements established by the university.

What is PESSAT? 

PESSAT stands for People’s Education Society Scholastic Aptitude Test, which is conducted by PES University to provide admission to B.Tech and other programs offered by PESIT. Based on their performance in the personal interview phase as well as their test results, candidates are shortlisted. 

PESSAT Registration Fee - Rs. 1150/-

What is KCET?

For Karnataka State Quota Seats, K-CET entrance tests and counselling are used to assign 45% of seats at PESIT South Campus and PES University.
