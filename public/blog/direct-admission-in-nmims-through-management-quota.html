How to Apply for Direct Admission in NMIMS through Management Quota?

NMIMS Direct Admission Process varies depending upon the program opted by the candidate. If a student want to take admission in any of the undergraduate programs, then in that case, a candidate has to clear an entrance exam named “NPAT” and accordingly on the basis candidates’s performance in NPAT, admission is granted.

For admission to postgraduate programs like MBA, candidate’s performance in “NMAT” by GMAC, followed by case discussion followed by personal interview as well. 
Talking of the admission to Ph.D progams, it is done through conducting of a specific entrance exam followed by an interview.

NPAT Exam - NPAT stands for National Test for Programs After Twelfth and it is conducted by Narsee Monjee Institute for Management Studies (NMIMS), Mumbai to admit students for undergraduate programs like BBA/B.Com/B.Sc etc.

NMAT Exam - NMAT is an important national-level MBA entrance exam that is necessary to clear in order to get admission in NMIMS Mumbai and other top colleges. In short, in order to apply for NMIMS management quota, primarily you need to clear the entrance exam and then you will be considered eligible to study MBA in NMIMS. After receiving a high score, you can contact us for a quick procedure. In addition to assisting you, we will also complete the direct admission in NMIMS through Management Quota on your behalf.

Direct Admission in NMIMS through Management Quota: Eligibility Criteria

To occupy a guaranteed seat through direct admission in NMIMS through Management Quota, here are some of the essential conditions to be fulfilled by every candidate wishing to pursue MBA. The eligibility criteria for NMIMS Direct Admission through management/nri quota is as follows -

  • The applicant must have earned a degree from an accredited college.
  • Student must have received a graduation grade of at least 50% in any discipline.
  • Students in their last academic year are also eligible to apply, however they must receive a final grade of at least 50% (45% for SC/ST).
  • Only a single mark sheet with all compulsory subjects passed is mandatory.
