West Bengal

West Bengal is one of the preferred states when it comes to Medical Admissions. Though the cost of education is on the higher side, the institutions of West Bengal are well-established with great infrastructure. There are a total of 19 Medical colleges with 4725 seats.  

West Bengal has 19 Government Medical Colleges, 6 Private Medical Colleges, and 1 AIIMS.  The process of selection includes, 

Details About West Bengal NEET MBBS Counselling 2023-

  • All India Quota counseling by the Medical Counselling Committee for 15% of seats of Government Medical Colleges.
  • The Department of Health & Family Welfare, Government of West Bengal, operates the West Bengal Medical Counseling Committee (WBMCC), which is responsible for conducting state quota counseling for 85% of government seats and private medical college seats in West Bengal. 

West Bengal is home to some of the top medical colleges in India, such as Calcutta Medical College, NRS Medical College, and the Institute of Postgraduate Medical Education and Research (IPGMER), to name a few. If you are aspiring to pursue MBBS in West Bengal and want to secure a seat, it is essential to have a clear understanding of the admission process. 

The first part of the MBBS admission in West Bengal is the West Bengal NEET MBBS Counseling is a process that starts just after the NEET result declaration. That Helps students allot seats in medical colleges across the state of West Bengal.

The West Bengal counselling process of NEET MBBS 2023 is conducted by the Department of Health and Family Welfare, Government of West Bengal. The West Bengal counselling process for admission to MBBS/BDS courses is generally conducted in multiple rounds. 

The first and second rounds are conducted through online means, while the third round, which is also known as the "Mop up" or spot round, is carried out offline. If seats are vacant after mop up then a college round will be conducted, also known as the “Stray round or College level round” To participate in the WEST BENGAL NEET MBBS COUNSELING process, students have to qualify NEET exam and then register themselves by login to its official website www.wbmcc.nic.in

Salient Features of West Bengal MBBS Counselling 2023

  • After the seat allotment, in WEST BENGAL NEET MBBS counseling, the process of document verification will take place.
  • In case an applicant fails to lock their choices, the system will do it automatically.
  • For every round of counseling, fresh registration will be opened.
  • Candidates who fail to report for admission after being allotted a seat in the first round will be disqualified from further rounds of counseling.
  • The verification of documents for NRI-allocated seats will take place at Swasthya Bhavan and not at the respective colleges.
  • Private medical colleges in West Bengal may require a bond and bank guarantee to be submitted at the time of reporting.
  • The process of document verification for NRI allotted seats will be conducted at Swasthya Bhavan, and not at the college.

Eligibility Criteria of West Bengal NEET Counselling

  • Mandatory for Students to Qualify for NEET.
  • To be eligible for claiming a seat under Government, Private, or Management quota, the candidate must be an Indian Citizen.
  • Foreign nationals can also apply for seats, but only under the NRI/Other Quota.
  • Candidates who are not domiciled in West Bengal are eligible to apply only for Management quota seats in Private Medical/Dental courses.
  • Domicile candidates are eligible to apply for Government, Government-aided Private, and Management quota seats.
  • Candidates must have successfully cleared their 12th grade or its equivalent exams to be eligible for admission into medical or dental courses.

Seat Matrix of West Bengal Counselling

The total Seat for MBBS in West Bengal is 4000 with total inclusion of Government and Private.  

In Government Colleges, There are 3150 seats-

  • 15 % of seats are reserved for the All India Quota which is 518 in number.
  • 85% for state Quota, that are 2572 seats in numbers.

There is a Total of 850 seats in Private institutes.

  • 283 seats are available under the State Quota.
  • The Management Quota has 437 seats on offer.
  • There are 130 seats available for NRI candidates.

 

Steps Involved In The West Bengal Counselling Process

  • You need to Register yourself,  online at https://wbmcc.nic.in/  your login id and password will be created
  • To register for West Bengal NEET MBBS Counseling, applicants are required to pay a registration fee of Rs. 2000 for the General category and Rs. 1500 for candidates belonging to reserved categories.
  • Login using your ID and password, and take a printout of your online application.

Then a merit list will be published

  • Fill online as per your choice.
  • Don’t forget to Lock your choices (The system won’t do it automatically), After this Final result will be declared.

Please submit a report to the Nodal Center with a detailed description of the tuition fee.

In Round 2, you have the option to either remain with the college you have been assigned or choose to upgrade to a different college.

  • Those who have registered in Round 1 do not need to register again in Round 2.

For Mop up, everyone has to apply including previously applied students who did not get a seat.
